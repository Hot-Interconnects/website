# Sponsor Talk

### Rohan Gahndi

Vice President of Optical Marketing at Eliyan

<img src="{{ 'assets/img/Rohan.Gandhi.eliyan.png'  | relative_url }}" alt="Rohan Gahndi" style="width:100%; max-width:250px; height:auto; object-fit:cover; object-position:top;">

**Title:** From Long Reach to Smart Reach: The Evolution of SerDes for AI Infrastructure

**Abstract:** 
<div style="text-align: justify; text-indent: 0em;">
<p>
The explosive growth of AI is reshaping the design priorities for high-speed interconnects. Unlike traditional networking applications that prioritize maximum reach, AI scale-up architectures demand unprecedented bandwidth density, lower power consumption, and optimized medium-reach connectivity between accelerators, memory, and emerging optical interfaces. This presentation examines why these fundamentally different system requirements call for a new generation of purpose-built SerDes architectures. Drawing on the development of a 224G PAM4 SerDes optimized specifically for AI infrastructure, the session explores the key architectural tradeoffs, implementation challenges and system-level benefits of designing for the unique characteristics of scale-up AI fabrics. You'll learn how optimized interconnect can increase aggregate bandwidth, improve power efficiency, and enable next-generation chiplet, near-package optics, and co-packaged optics deployments.
</p>

</div>
<br>

**Biography:** 
<div style="text-align: justify; text-indent: 0em;">
<p>Rohan Gandhi is Vice President of Optical Marketing at Eliyan, where he leads product definition and strategy for next-generation optical interconnect solutions. He is a technology leader with over 20 years of experience developing mixed-signal and optical products. With prior roles at Marvell, Inphi, and MACOM. he has driven product definition and market development for 100G, 400G, and 800G Optical DSPs. 
</p>
<p>
Rohan began his career at the Ultrafast Photonics Laboratory at the Georgia Institute of Technology, where he worked on VCSEL-based optical systems. He holds an M.S. in Electrical Engineering from Georgia Tech and a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Mumbai.
</p>
</div>
